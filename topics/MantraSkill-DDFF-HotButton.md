### Highest-Leverage Motivation

**Primary emotional driver:** Fear of invisibility (F3 — ATS machine never shows CV to human) + Fear of wasted effort (F1 — 15,981 lamaran → 0 offer)

**Evidence:** Both appear across multiple independent Threads posts. Ghosting (FR1) is the emotional trigger that gets people to engage. ATS score gap (D5) is the rational explanation that makes the system feel "solvable."

**Messaging implication:**
- Lead with "mesin" (ATS) framing — it's the enemy they can now beat
- Use specific numbers (35→87) not vague promises ("improved CV")
- The 850+ alumni is social proof that the system works
- "Bukan luck, tapi sistem" = the core reframe from helpless to empowered

## Hot-Button Map

*(Framework from Pawbytes skill-suites — adapted for MantraSkill)*

### Asset Type: Organic Facebook/Threads Post

**Desired action:** Click link → sign up for free trial

**Primary DDFF input:** F3 (ATS machine doesn't show CV to human) + D5 (ATS score is solvable)

**Reptilian hot button:** Status threat + loss aversion
- Fear: CV never gets read by human → application effort is wasted
- Urgency: Every week without ATS optimization = more ghosting
- Risk: Continue same approach = same 0 callback result

**Limbic hot button:** Relief + competence restoration
- Identity: "I'm not incapable, the system was just broken for me"
- Belonging: 850+ alumni who figured it out
- Mastery: 7 days to understand the system, not months of guessing

## Copy Guardrails for MantraSkill

### ✅ Do

- Use "mesin"/"ATS" as the external enemy (not the user)
- Specific numbers: 35→87, 850+, 7 hari
- Personal pronoun: "gue", "lo", "kami"
- "Bukan luck, tapi sistem" — empowerment reframe
- Screenshot ATS scores as visual proof
- Acknowledge ghosting pain before offering solution

### ❌ Don't

- Don't blame the user ("CV lo jelek")
- Don't use vague claims ("perbaiki CV lo")
- Don't brand voice ("MantraSkill presents...")
- Don't use hype language ("REVOLUSIONARY!")
- Don't dismiss emotions ("cuma masalah attitude")
- Don't compare to random success ("like our CEO...")
